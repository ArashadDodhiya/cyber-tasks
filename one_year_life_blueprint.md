# 🧠 BRANCH 1: MENTAL GAME & MINDSET

## The Psychology of Career Switching Without a Safety Net

### Month 1-3: Survival Mode → Builder Mode
The hardest part isn't learning — it's the voice that says "did I make a mistake?"

**Rules for this phase:**
- Do NOT compare your timeline to anyone else's
- Treat your learning like a full-time job (8 hours/day, 5 days/week)
- Track progress weekly — your brain lies about how far you've come
- Talk to 1 person in cybersecurity every week (even online)

### Month 4-6: Builder Mode → Proof Mode
- Imposter syndrome will peak here — you'll know enough to see how much you don't know
- This is normal. Every senior pentester went through this
- Focus: "What can I show?" not "What do I know?"

### Month 7-12: Proof Mode → Professional Mode
- You'll start getting interviews or bounty results
- Rejection is data, not judgment
- Every "no" teaches you what to prepare better

### Daily Mental Health Habits
| Habit | Why | Time |
|-------|-----|------|
| Morning walk (30 min) | Clears anxiety, improves focus | 6:30 AM |
| Journal 3 things learned | Proves progress to yourself | Night |
| No social media first 2 hours | Protects your focus from comparison | Morning |
| 1 day off per week | Prevents burnout completely | Sunday |
| Talk to family/friend about wins | External validation helps early on | Evening |
