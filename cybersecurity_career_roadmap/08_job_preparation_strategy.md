## 🎯 Entry-Level Roles to Target (In Order of Fit)

### Tier 1: Best Fit for Your Background (Apply Month 5-7)

#### 1. Application Security Analyst / Engineer
**Why this is your #1 target:** Your dev background + security skills = perfect match. This is the role where you have the LEAST competition because most security candidates can't read code.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Salary Range (India) | ₹4-8 LPA (fresher/junior), ₹8-15 LPA (2-3 years exp) |
| Key Skills | Code review, SAST/DAST, OWASP Top 10, secure SDLC, API security |
| Tools to Know | Semgrep, SonarQube, Checkmarx, Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP |
| Your Edge | You've BUILT apps with Spring Boot + Angular. You understand code architecture. |
| Companies (India) | Flipkart, Paytm, Razorpay, Zomato, CRED, Swiggy, any tech company with a security team |
| Remote Possibility | Medium-High (many tech companies offer hybrid/remote) |

#### 2. Junior Penetration Tester / VAPT Analyst
**The classic pentesting role.** Most Indian security consulting firms hire juniors.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Salary Range (India) | ₹3-6 LPA (fresher), ₹6-10 LPA (2 years exp) |
| Key Skills | Web app pentesting, network pentesting, AD attacks, report writing |
| Tools to Know | Nmap, Burp Suite, Metasploit, BloodHound, Nessus/OpenVAS |
| Your Edge | Lab experience, documented methodology, report writing skills |
| Companies (India) | Payatu, Sequretek, SAFE Security, Aujas, Pristine InfoSolutions, Suma Soft, Cipher, Ernst & Young, Deloitte, KPMG, PwC (Big 4 cybersecurity divisions) |
| Remote Possibility | Low-Medium (most consulting firms want on-site initially) |

#### 3. DevSecOps Engineer
**Bridge between dev and security.** Growing demand in India.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Salary Range (India) | ₹5-10 LPA (junior), ₹10-20 LPA (2-3 years) |
| Key Skills | CI/CD security, Docker security, IaC scanning, SAST/DAST integration |
| Tools to Know | Jenkins/GitHub Actions, Docker, Terraform, Trivy, Snyk, SonarQube |
| Your Edge | Dev background means you understand the pipeline. Most security people don't. |
| Companies | Any company with DevOps teams (which is most tech companies now) |
| Remote Possibility | High (DevOps culture is remote-friendly) |

### Tier 2: Stepping Stone Roles (Apply Month 7-9, if Tier 1 isn't working)

#### 4. SOC Analyst (Level 1)
**Not your dream role, but gets your foot in the door.** Plan to transition to pentesting within 1-2 years.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Salary Range (India) | ₹2.5-4.5 LPA (fresher), ₹4-7 LPA (1-2 years) |
| Key Skills | SIEM (Splunk/ELK), log analysis, incident triage, alert handling |
| Additional Learning | 2-3 weeks of Splunk basics, understanding of MITRE ATT&CK |
| Your Edge | Your offensive knowledge helps you understand what attacks look like |
| Companies | Wipro, TCS, Infosys, HCLTech (cybersecurity divisions), CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks |
| Remote Possibility | Low (most SOC roles require on-site/shift work) |
| Exit Strategy | 12-18 months in SOC → internal transfer to pentesting or external move |

#### 5. Security Analyst (GRC/Compliance)
**Least exciting but most available.** Consider only as absolute backup.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Salary Range (India) | ₹3-5 LPA (fresher) |
| Key Skills | ISO 27001, NIST, compliance auditing, risk assessment |
| Learning Needed | 1 month of framework study |
| Your Edge | Low, but it's a job |
| Exit Strategy | Use this to fund certifications, continue pentesting on the side |

**Hard truth about salaries:**
- LinkedIn posts showing ₹20-30 LPA for freshers are OUTLIERS, not norms
- Most entry-level cybersecurity in India pays ₹3-6 LPA
- Your dev experience may help negotiate 10-20% above pure freshers
- Consulting firms generally pay less than product companies
- Remote international work can pay 2-5x Indian market rates (but harder to get)

### Where to Apply
| Platform | Priority | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| LinkedIn | 🔴 Critical | Most cybersecurity jobs posted here. Apply + message recruiter. |
| Naukri | 🔴 Critical | India's largest job portal. Update profile for security keywords. |
| Company Career Pages | 🔴 Critical | Direct applications bypass recruiter filters sometimes. |
| Indeed India | 🟡 Important | Good for consulting firm roles. |
| Twitter/X | 🟡 Important | Many security jobs posted informally. Follow #infosecjobs #cybersecurityjobs. |
| Instahyre | 🟢 Useful | Curated job platform. |
| AngelList / Wellfound | 🟢 Useful | Startup security roles. |
| Referrals | 🔴 Critical | **#1 way to get interviews.** Build your network. |

### The Referral Strategy
1. Connect with 5 security professionals per week on LinkedIn
2. Engage with their posts genuinely (not spammy)
3. After 2-3 weeks of interaction, ask: "I'm transitioning into cybersecurity from dev. Would you be open to a 15-minute call about your experience?"
4. On the call, ask about their company's hiring process
5. After building rapport, ask if they'd be willing to refer you when a position opens
6. **50% of cybersecurity jobs are filled through referrals.** Your network IS your net worth.

## 🎭 Interview Preparation

### What Indian Cybersecurity Interviews Test

| Interview Stage | What They Test | How to Prepare |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| HR Screening | Communication, salary expectations, certifications | Practice your "why security" story. Know market salary. |
| Technical Round 1 | OWASP Top 10, networking basics, Linux commands | Review your interview prep docs. Practice explaining verbally. |
| Technical Round 2 | Hands-on demo or scenario-based questions | Be ready to demo Burp Suite, explain an attack chain, solve a CTF challenge. |
| Practical Test | Given a vulnerable machine/app to pentest in 2-4 hours | Practice on HTB machines under time pressure. Write a report. |
| Manager Round | Methodology, report writing, teamwork, career goals | Explain your methodology clearly. Show a sample report. |

### Your "Why Security" Story (Practice This Until It's Natural)
```
"I spent [X years] building web applications with Spring Boot and Angular. 
I understood how software is built — the architecture, the database, the APIs.

But I kept finding myself more interested in what could go WRONG with these 
systems than what went right. I started studying OWASP, set up labs, practiced 
on PortSwigger and Hack The Box.

I realized: I don't just want to build applications. I want to make them SECURE. 
My development background means I can review code, understand business logic 
flaws, and find vulnerabilities that traditional pentesters miss.

I'm not a developer who couldn't find a dev job. I'm a developer who CHOSE 
security because I believe building something meaningful means making it safe."
```

### Top 20 Interview Questions to Prepare

| # | Question | What They're Really Asking |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Explain your pentesting methodology | Do you have a structured approach or do you randomly poke? |
| 2 | What is SQL injection? How do you test for it? | Can you explain technical concepts clearly? |
| 3 | Difference between XSS types (reflected, stored, DOM) | Do you understand nuances? |
| 4 | What is SSRF? How is it dangerous? | Can you explain impact, not just definition? |
| 5 | How would you pentest a web application? | Give them a step-by-step methodology |
| 6 | What tools do you use for web pentesting? | Burp Suite fluency is mandatory |
| 7 | Explain Active Directory attack chain | Can you chain attacks, not just use individual tools? |
| 8 | What is Pass-the-Hash? How does it work? | Do you understand the underlying protocol? |
| 9 | How do you escalate privileges on Linux? | Practical knowledge, not just theory |
| 10 | What is the difference between vulnerability assessment and penetration testing? | Do you understand the industry? |
| 11 | How do you write a pentest report? | Can you communicate findings to non-technical people? |
| 12 | What is the MITRE ATT&CK framework? | Are you aware of industry frameworks? |
| 13 | How do you stay updated with security news? | Are you genuinely interested or just studying for the interview? |
| 14 | Tell me about a vulnerability you found | Have you actually DONE anything? (This is where your labs matter) |
| 15 | What is IDOR and how do you test for it? | Practical testing knowledge |
| 16 | Explain how JWT works. How can it be attacked? | Modern app security knowledge |
| 17 | What is Kerberoasting? | AD knowledge depth |
| 18 | How would you test an API for security issues? | API security is increasingly important |
| 19 | What's the difference between SAST and DAST? | AppSec knowledge (your edge!) |
| 20 | Why should we hire you over someone with a certification? | Your answer: practical skills, dev background, portfolio of proof |

## 📊 Hiring Timeline Expectations (Be Patient)

| Action | Typical Response Time |
|--------|----------------------|
| Apply → HR screening call | 1-3 weeks (many never respond) |
| HR call → Technical interview | 1-2 weeks |
| Technical interview → Practical test | 1 week |
| Practical test → Offer | 1-3 weeks |
| **Total: Application → Offer** | **4-8 weeks** |
| **Realistic applications needed for 1 offer** | **50-100+ applications** |

> **Hard truth:** In India, expect to apply to 100+ positions before getting your first cybersecurity job offer. This is normal. Don't get discouraged at application #30.
