# 🎯 Learning Order & Priorities

> Not everything matters equally. This document ranks skills by employability impact so you don't waste time on low-value activities.

---

## Priority Tier System

### 🔴 TIER 1: LEARN FIRST (Month 1-4) — Directly Gets You Hired

These skills are required for 80%+ of entry-level cybersecurity job postings in India.

| Skill | Why It's Critical | Time Needed | You're Currently At |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|--------------------|
| Web Application Pentesting (deep) | #1 demanded skill for pentesting roles. Your dev background makes this your strongest weapon. | 2-3 months to master | 40% — basics done, need depth |
| Professional Report Writing | Every pentester must write reports. Companies judge you on report quality. | 1-2 months practice | 20% — need professional templates |
| Active Directory Attacks | Required for internal pentesting. Most interview scenarios involve AD. | You have good foundation. 1 month to polish. | 55% — good but need speed |
| Linux/Windows Privilege Escalation | Core pentesting skill. Always asked in interviews. | You have documentation. 2 weeks to sharpen. | 60% — review and speed up |
| Networking Fundamentals (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP) | Foundation for everything. If you don't understand packets, you can't pentest. | Review only — you have basics. | 50% |
| Burp Suite (advanced) | Primary tool for web pentesting. Must be expert-level. | 1-2 months of daily use | 40% — need advanced features |

### 🟡 TIER 2: LEARN SECOND (Month 5-8) — Differentiates You From Other Candidates

| Skill | Why It Matters | Time Needed | Current Level |
|-------|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| Source Code Review / SAST | Your dev background is your edge. NO other candidate offers this. | 1-2 months | 10% — need structured practice |
| API Security Testing | Modern apps are API-heavy. Growing demand. | 1 month | 15% |
| Cloud Security (AWS basics) | Every job posting mentions "cloud awareness". You don't need deep expertise, just basics. | 1 month | 0% |
| Tool Building (Python) | Proves you can code AND hack. Separates you from script kiddies. | 1-2 months | 30% (you can code, just need security focus) |
| Docker/Container Security | Modern DevSecOps skill. Increasingly required. | 2-3 weeks | 5% |
| One certification (eJPT or Sec+) | Passes HR filters. Gets your resume to human reviewers. | 1-2 months prep | 0% |

### 🟢 TIER 3: LEARN LATER (Month 9-12+) — Nice to Have, Not Urgent

| Skill | Why It Can Wait | When to Learn |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| Exploit Development / Buffer Overflow | Very few entry-level jobs require this. Important for OSCP but not for getting hired. | Month 11+ or Year 2 |
| Reverse Engineering / Malware Analysis | Specialized skill. Only needed for specific roles (malware analyst, threat researcher). | Year 2 |
| Red Team Operations (C2, evasion) | Senior-level skill. You need 2+ years of pentesting first. | Year 2-3 |
| OSCP Certification | Expensive (₹1.5L+). Requires strong fundamentals. Don't rush this. | Year 2 (when you can afford it + have skills) |
| Blockchain/Smart Contract Security | Niche. Very few jobs in India. | Only if specifically interested |
| Mobile Pentesting (Android/iOS) | Growing field but fewer entry-level openings. | Year 2 |
| AI/ML Security | Emerging field. Not enough jobs yet for entry level. | Year 2+ |

### ❌ IGNORE COMPLETELY (For Now)

These are common time-wasters for beginners:

| Skill/Activity | Why to Skip | What People Say vs Reality |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| CEH Certification | Expensive (₹30-50K), poor reputation among actual professionals, teaches outdated concepts. HR values it but professionals mock it. | "You need CEH" → False. eJPT or Sec+ is better ROI. |
| Learning 10 programming languages | You already know Java, JavaScript, SQL. That's enough. Add Python for scripting. Stop there. | "Learn Rust/Go/C++" → Not now. Python + Bash is enough. |
| Building a SOC home lab with Splunk/ELK | Only if you're pursuing blue team. For pentesting, this is a distraction. | "Set up a SIEM" → Only if applying for SOC roles. |
| Collecting certifications without practice | 3 certs + no hands-on = unemployable. 0 certs + strong portfolio = hireable. | "Get Security+, CEH, CySA+" → Get ONE cert + build proof instead. |
| Advanced cryptography theory | Understand basics (hashing, encryption, TLS). Don't study math-heavy crypto. | "Learn how RSA works mathematically" → Know what it does, not the math. |
| Spending months on Kali Linux customization | Your tools work fine. Stop tweaking. Start hacking. | "Perfect your Kali setup" → Use defaults + start practicing. |
| Watching YouTube tutorials endlessly | After 3-4 months, you know enough to practice. Tutorial watching is procrastination disguised as learning. | "I'll watch one more video" → No. Open a lab and DO it. |

## 🛑 What You Should STOP Doing Right Now

1. **Stop watching tutorial videos for topics you already understand.** You know Linux basics, networking basics, OWASP basics. You don't need another video on "What is SQL Injection." Go DO the PortSwigger labs instead.

2. **Stop collecting resources.** You have enough bookmarks. Use: PortSwigger, HTB, TryHackMe, and your home lab. That's it for platforms.

3. **Stop studying topics "just in case."** If it's not in Tier 1 or Tier 2, it can wait.

4. **Stop perfecting your notes.** Your cyber-tasks repo has 100+ docs. Good enough. Start turning knowledge into PROOF (blogs, tools, reports).

5. **Stop comparing yourself to people on Twitter.** That person who found an RCE on their first bug bounty? They probably had 2 years of experience before they started hunting. Your timeline is YOUR timeline.

6. **Stop avoiding job applications.** "I'm not ready" is almost always fear, not reality. Start applying at Month 5 even if you feel 70% ready.

---

## 📌 The 80/20 Rule for Cybersecurity Jobs in India

**80% of entry-level job interviews will test these 5 things:**

1. Can you find and exploit web vulnerabilities? (OWASP Top 10 + Burp Suite)
2. Can you explain your pentesting methodology step by step?
3. Can you write a professional pentest report?
4. Do you understand network protocols and how attacks work at the packet level?
5. Can you demonstrate practical skills (live demo, CTF challenge, or walkthrough)?

**Focus on these 5 things. Everything else is secondary for getting your FIRST job.**
